Blindness and Vision Impairment

The International Classification of Diseases-10 (Update and Revision 2006) delineates four distinct levels of visual function. These include normal vision, moderate visual impairment, severe visual impairment, and blindness. The categories of moderate visual impairment and severe visual impairment are collectively classified under the term "low vision." Consequently, the combined spectrum of low vision and blindness encompasses all forms of visual impairment.

Around The World 285 million people are estimated to be visually impaired worldwide: 39 million are blind and 246 have low vision

Jordan Over 475,000 people in Jordan are living with blindness.

Visual Impairment • About 90% of the world's visually impaired live in developing countries

Disability The most common disability (difficulties) among Jordanians aged 5 and above is visual impairment (difficulty of vision).
